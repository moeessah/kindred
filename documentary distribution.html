SubjectHe got up before his son every morning for seventeen years.

Hi Lisa,

His name is David Kahananui. He lives in Kaneohe, on the windward side of O'ahu. He researched the charter school, drove to the information night, filed the application, and followed up twice. What Archer got to be asked, David never was.

Archer graduated from the Hawaiian Academy of Arts and Science in 2023. He used Kindred's platform in class the year before and saw, for the first time, careers he had never encountered growing up. For David, the same discovery arrived thirty years too late.

We made a film about David. Twenty-two minutes. It is called AVAILABLE.

Watch AVAILABLE

There are a lot of Davids. People who did the responsible thing, built a life, and still never got asked what kind of life they wanted. Every student who uses Kindred is one fewer person left to find out thirty years late. Your gift last year kept that work running in classrooms where districts cannot cover the licence fee. Thank you for that.

A gift before 31 December keeps Kindred in twelve classrooms through May.

Podcast Outreach

Six shows.
Different question each time.

Chris does not pitch the film. He pitches what it uncovered, and every host gets a different version of it. A workforce researcher hears one thing. A CTE teacher hears another. A documentary programmer hears something else. The pitch changes. The film does not.

The show talks to Chris. The audience finds David.

Pitch formula
SubjectOne sentence: Chris's name, Kindred, the film. Nothing else.
AngleWhat the film means for their specific audience. Not "a great fit for your show."
AssetsFilm link, 60-word bio, three talking points. No deck.
Follow-upOnce, day 10. Plain text. No second pitch.

Festival Circuit

One premiere.
Protected.

Short documentary at 22 minutes has a specific circuit. Tribeca and Full Frame are the credibility anchors in the US. Hot Docs brings the international conversation and a market. SXSW EDU is the room where the people who decide what goes into schools are physically present.

Don't burn the premiere online before the film has had a room. One festival, protect it. Educational distribution opens after that: Kanopy, Docuseek, the Video Project.

Submission basics
PremiereHold the US premiere for Tribeca or Full Frame. Not both simultaneously.
PlatformFilmFreeway for most submissions. Tribeca has its own Extranet.
ScreenerVimeo. Password-protected, unchanging through review.
DisclosureAll submissions note that Kindred's platform appears in the film and that Kindred is the producing organisation.

Kindred is a 501(c)(3). Sundance Documentary Fund (open call closes June 15, 2026), NEH Public Programs, and the Fledgling Fund all have eligibility criteria the organisation meets. The grant strategy starts during pre-production, not after the edit is finished.

Screens annually, typically spring

SXSW EDU
Austin, Texas. The annual gathering for education innovation. Attendees include district administrators, edtech funders, and curriculum directors: the people who decide what resources reach CTE teachers. A film that screens here is not in front of an audience that loves documentary. It is in front of an audience that makes purchasing decisions.
Format: Submit as a session, not only as a film. Essah and Chris speak on what the film revealed about what students are shown versus what their parents were never offered.
